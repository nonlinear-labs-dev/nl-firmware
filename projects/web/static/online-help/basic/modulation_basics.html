Taking the simplest approach to the concept of the C15 modulation mechanism would be the following example.

We consider a Macro Control and a target parameter. The Macro Control and the parameter itself are unipolar, the modulation amount is bipolar. Furthermore, let the Macro Control be assigned to the parameter.

Macro Control
50.0 %
Mod Amount
0.0 %
Param
50.0 %

Depending on the parameter's modulation amount, the parameter will follow weighted movements of the Macro Control, as long as the weighted movement fits inside the parameter's operating range. Negative modulation amounts will invert the direction of the weighted movement.

When adjusting a particular control, the different effects of Macro Controls, modulation amounts and target parameters will become quite obvious. Adjusting a parameter will not just change its position, the edges of the modulation range will change as well. Adjusting the modulation amount will only change the parameter's modulation range and edges. When the Macro Control is changed, the parameter position will change according to the modulation range.
