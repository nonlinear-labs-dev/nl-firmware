Shaper Basics

back to Shaper

Shaper units provide the possibility to distort signals. In the C15, shapers are implemented for each Oscillator, Mixer and the Cabinet unit. They can be mixed (cross-faded) onto the actual signal. The shaping algorithm is defined by three Parameters: Drive, Fold and Asymmetry. Depending on Fold, the shaping effect can be a mixture of saturation and a periodic folding, both having their individual effects on the signal's harmonics.

Drive

Input gain (in dB) of the sine shaper stage. Higher gains will create more distortion and harmonics. Shapers have a drive range of 50 dB.

Fold

Amount of folding back of the shaper curve for high input amplitudes. A higher amount of folding leads to a softer but more nasal sound, as the signal becomes folded back into a periodic sine curve.

Asymmetry

Asymmetry of the shaper curve, generating even (2nd, 4th, ...) harmonics. At higher values it becomes a parabolic curve that shifts the frequency of the fundamental to its double.
